About Mahatma Gandhi

  • Mahatma Gandhi full name is Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

  • 1869 - He was born in 2 October 1869 Porbandar, Kathiawar Agency, British India

  • Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the Father of the Nation, was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist

  • 1891 - at the age of 22 Gandhi trained in the law at the Inner Temple in London, and was called to the bar

  • 1893 - After two uncertain years in India, where he was unable to start a successful law practice, he moved to South Africa to represent an Indian merchant in a lawsuit.

  • 1915 -He went on to live in South Africa for 21 years. he returned to India and soon set about organising peasants, farmers, and urban labourers to protest against discrimination and excessive land-tax.

  • 1921 -Assuming leadership of the Indian National Congress Gandhi led nationwide campaigns for easing poverty, expanding women's rights, building religious and ethnic amity, ending untouchability, and, above all, achieving swaraj or self-rule.

  • 1930 -Bringing anti-colonial nationalism to the common Indians, Gandhi led them in challenging the British-imposed salt tax with the 400 km (250 mi) Dandi Salt March

  • 1940 -Gandhi's vision of an independent India based on religious pluralism was challenged in the early 1940s by a Muslim nationalism which demanded a separate homeland for Muslims within British India

  • 1942 -calling for the British to quit India He was imprisoned many times and for many years in both South Africa and India.

  • who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule.

  • 1947In August 1947, Britain granted independence, but the British Indian Empire was partitioned into two dominions, a Hindu-majority India and a Muslim-majority Pakistan.

  • 1948 -when he was 78. The belief that Gandhi had been too resolute in his defense of both Pakistan and Indian Muslims spread among some Hindus in India. Among these was Nathuram Godse, a militant Hindu nationalist from Pune, western India, who assassinated Gandhi by firing three bullets into his chest at an interfaith prayer meeting in Delhi on 30 January 1948.

  • He is known for his principles of truth and non-violence (Satyagraha), which he used to inspire movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

  • His legacy continues to inspire millions globally, and his birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated as the International Day of Non-Violence.
